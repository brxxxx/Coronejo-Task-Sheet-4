The Privacy Journey

Trace the development of privacy rights from the earliest concepts to modern-day global regulations.

1890
Global (USA)

The Right to Privacy

Samuel Warren and Louis Brandeis published "The Right to Privacy" in the Harvard Law Review, introducing the concept of “the right to be let alone.”

1948
Global (UN)

Universal Declaration of Human Rights

Adopted by the UN after WWII, Article 12 protects individuals’ privacy and recognizes it as a basic human right.

1974
Global (USA)

Privacy Act of 1974

Regulates how U.S. government agencies handle personal data, giving individuals access to their records and allowing corrections.

1981
Treaty

Convention 108

The first international treaty on data protection, setting standards for handling personal data and open to countries beyond Europe.

1995
Europe

Data Protection Directive

Standardized data protection across EU countries and introduced consent and data security principles (95/46/EC).

2012
🇵🇭
Philippines

Data Privacy Act (RA 10173)

Protects personal data in the Philippines and created the National Privacy Commission (NPC) to enforce data protection rules.

2016
Global (EU)

General Data Protection Regulation

Strengthened data protection across the EU, introduced strict rules on data handling, and enforced heavy penalties for violations.

2021
🇵🇭
Philippines

NPC Circulars and Guidelines Updates

Updated rules under the Data Privacy Act, strengthening data breach reporting and improved enforcement of privacy laws.

The Horizon of Privacy

As technology evolves, the way we protect our digital identity is transforming. Here's what's coming next.

AI Governance

Regulation will focus on algorithmic transparency and mandatory audits for automated decisions.

Children's Digital Rights

Strict age verification and age-appropriate design codes for social platforms.

Privacy Litigation

Surge in lawsuits targeting tracking pixels and deceptive data practices.

Data Sovereignty

Fractured data flows as nations prioritize local security over global transfers.

Standardized PETs

Gold-standard anonymization using synthetic data and federated learning.

Cross-Watchdog Cooperation

Oversight from privacy, antitrust, and cybersecurity regulators working together.
